Ecosystem Destruction

Coral Bleaching in the Great Barrier Reef

Another terrible impact of climate change is its affect on the marine wildlife. Specifically, high CO2 levels causes the ocean to absorb excess carbon, creating a phenomena known as ocean acidification. Additionally, the high C02 levels also raise the temperature of the ocean water.

One effect of ocean acidification and warming has been the death of coral reef colonies. The warmer temperatures cause the coral to reject the algae that gives the coral its color and energy. Known as "coral bleaching", this term refers to the color of coral reefs turning to white as they die. This phenomena has been seen in the Great Barrier Reef as scores of reef colonies die.
